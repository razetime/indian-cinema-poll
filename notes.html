Technical Notes

The code and data for this website project is hosted on GitHub.

The data from the poll results can be seen in data.csv, in its raw format.

The data is parsed in conv.rb, and the movie and voter data is stored as prolog data in data2.P. Prolog is a programming language that I find is good for reasoning and generating text.

Generation of the movie related pages (ranking, who voted) is done in genm.P and voter related pages (list, personal notes) in genv.P.

The final list is ranked using a Borda Count ranking system, which is a facy way to say that the first movie in your list gets 10 points, the next one 9 points, and so on. This system is better when the voter to movie ratio is around 1/5, the ranking hence may be somewhat skewed. But then again, ranking movies using numbers or other objective isn't particularly accurate anyway. I hope the results are not too controversial.

This project was made over the course of three days, where I certainly realized that my prolog skills were inadequate for web oriented work. It was certainly newer to me than I expected, and not as easy as I thought. However, it was still very fun to do and I'm very happy that I was able to do some coding work that wasn't the usual stuff I deal with in academia.

Some portions of the site and its design have come out a bit amateurish due to real life stress. I am still open to any suggestions or problem reports on Discord.

I hope I can run this poll again next year, in better form, with more voters, with more beautiful entries of Indian cinema to explore in the endless sea. May there be a free Palestine the next time we run this poll.
